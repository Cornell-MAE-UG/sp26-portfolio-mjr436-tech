For class, we were asked to optimize the design of a nutcracker to best crack a nut given some specifications on our assignment.

Problem Statement: we need to determine the optimal or necessary dimensions of a nutcracker of our design right when the nutcracker is about to crack a nut.

Constraints and Input Parameters: The assignment provided us enough information to determine that the necessary force or load required to actually crack the nut is around 250kg. Since the average grip strength for a human being is averaged at around 40kg, considering differences across gender and age, we can use this value for the average load applied. Additionally, we can assume the nut is 2cm.

Approach to the Problem: There are a few necessary quantitative steps that must be taken to determine the design dimensions of our nutcracker. First, a free body diagram must be drawn to orient the design and allow us to quantitatively analyze it. Firstly, we balanced the moment using the joint of the design as the origin and assuming that the device was in static equilibrium. This allowed us to solve for some of the relevant dimensions, ultimately yielding the dimensions 56cm by 19.2cm. 

Image: ![Nutcracker](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/0b75f528-b630-442c-b475-b2363c8439b0)

Discussion: This design is sincerely not functional considering its size. A person would not be able to properly use it effectively, and therefore to maximize efficiency in the future, the dimensions would have to be restricted somehow.
